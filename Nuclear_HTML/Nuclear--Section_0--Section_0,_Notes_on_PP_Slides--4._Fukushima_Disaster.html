4. Fukushima Disaster

Fukushima 1
• 6 x BWR (Gen II)
• Total output of 4.7GW (one of the largest 25 worldwide)
• Commissioned in 1971
• Suffered damage on 11/3/2011 due to earthquake
• 2/6 reactors were shutdown at the time and a further was de-fuelled
• Reactors were shut down following earthquake using control rods
• Other power stations were shutdown and power was lost.
• Fission ceased but heat was still created due to radioactivity in core so constant cooling water was required to prevent overheating

Core Heating
• Cooled using electric pumps to circulate cooling water
• Loss of power led to shutdown of cooling system
• Backup generators cut in but stopped working around an hour later (presumed water damage)
• Second battery backup used but designed to last 8 hours
• Engineers improvised by pumping seawater into the core through fire water pumps

Hydrogen Explosions
• Seawater flow rate was insufficient for cooling the core.
• Water boiled to steam,
• Fuel rods were exposed to air
• Zirconium fuel cladding melted and reacted with steam to form hydrogen
• This built up in the reactors and caused 2 explosions (in buildings 1&3)
• This may have caused further damage to fire water pumps supplying seawater

Meltdown
• Fuel was in ceramic form so melting was not expected
• massive heat caused radioactive elements to leach from ceramic fuel pellets
• In a meltdown, molten core melts though the steel containment vessel and is leaked into the environment
• Meltdown occurred in 3/6 reactors (1,2&3)

Impact
• Dangerous radioactive caesium levels observed up to 30-50km from the plant
• a 12 mile exclusion zone is still in effect
• 10s of thousands of residents may never be able to return home
• Japans use of nuclear power decreased dramatically (only 2 now in operation)
