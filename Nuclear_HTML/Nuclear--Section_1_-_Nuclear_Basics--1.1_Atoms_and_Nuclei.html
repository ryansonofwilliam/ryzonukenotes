1.1 Atoms and Nuclei

Atomic & Nuclear Structure
• Atom - smallest unit of chemical structure
• Z - Electrons - Each has a charge of 1.602x10^-19 Coulomb
• Nucleus is Tiny - 100,000 times smaller than overall atom size BUT has 99.9% of the mass of the atom
• if element is Non-Ionic, element has Z electrons and Protons
• Proton has +1e positive charge.
• Neutron has neutral charge
• Both have mass of 983.28 MeV/c2
• protons and neutrons repel as different charge but are hel together by Strong Nuclear Force
• Z = Protons (Atomic Number), Defines element
• N = Neutrons (Neutron Number), defines isotope
• A = Atomic Mass (number of Nucleons in the nucleus) (N+Z)

Structure of Nucleus
• Quarks make Neutrons and Protons
• Cloud of electrons surround nucleus

Radioactivity
• Ernest Rutherford determined that there were 3 types of radioactivity, Alpha Beta Gamma
• Alpha decay is where a Helium nucleus is ejected from larger atom, changing the original element.
• Beta decay is where an electron and other energy is emitted
• Gamma Decay is where high energy nuclear photon is emitted from nucleus.

Special Relativity
• E=mc2 c = 2.998x108
• Mass Defect - change in mass is related to change in energy
• 1eV = 1.602x1019 joule - energy required to move single electronic charge through 1v of potential difference

☐ Nuclear strong force is strong but short range.


Atomic Mass Number is not exactly the atomic mass
